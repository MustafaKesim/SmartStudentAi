Formula 1 (F1) is the world's top-tier open-wheel, single-seater racing car championship. Supervised by the International Automobile Federation (FIA), it is generally considered the pinnacle of motorsport.

Key Features
Cars: These specially manufactured cars are extremely lightweight, aerodynamic, and can reach speeds of 360 km/h. Races: Each race, called a "Grand Prix," is held on private racetracks or street circuits around the world. Championships: Throughout a season, drivers accumulate points for the "Drivers' Championship," and teams for the "Constructors' Championship."

How the Weekend Goes?
Practice: Free practice laps are held on Friday and Saturday to adjust the cars.
Qualifying: On Saturday, qualifying laps are held to determine the starting grid for Sunday's race. Race: This is the main event held on Sundays and concludes with the cars covering a distance of approximately 305 km (excluding Monaco).
