The FIFA World Cup is an international football tournament contested by the senior men's national teams of the members of the Federation Internationale
 de Football Association (FIFA). The tournament has been held every four years since 1930, except in 1942 and 1946 when it was not held because of the 
 Second World War.

The competition consists of two parts: the qualification phase and the final phase, officially called the World Cup Finals. The qualification phase, 
which currently takes place over the preceding three years, is used to determine which teams qualify for the final phase. In the final phase, 32 teams 
compete for the title at venues within the host nation over about a month.

Brazil is the most successful national team, having won the tournament five times. Other former champions include Germany and Italy, with four titles 
each, and Argentina and France, with two titles each. The World Cup is the most prestigious association football tournament in the world, as well as 
the most widely viewed and followed single sporting event in the world.
